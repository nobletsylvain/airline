# Historical Airlines Database

**Version:** 0.1  
**Date:** January 2026  
**Companion to:** Game Design Document v0.7, Data Model v0.5, Economic Parameters v0.1

---

## Overview

This document provides seed data for historical scenarios and "Take the Helm" mode. Each entry includes starting position (fleet, network, finances), historical context, key events, and player challenges.

**Design Goal:** Let players rewrite aviation history. Can you save Pan Am? Can you grow Southwest faster? Can you navigate deregulation better than Braniff?

> **Economic Context:** All prices, costs, and revenues use era-accurate values. See economic-parameters.md for aircraft pricing by year, fuel costs, labor indices, and economic calibration data.

## 1. American Carriers — Deregulation Era

### 1.1 Pan American World Airways (1978)

**The Scenario:** "The Fall of an Empire"

**Starting Position:**
| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Year | 1978 |
| Fleet | 127 aircraft |
| Hubs | JFK (primary), Miami, San Francisco |
| Annual Revenue | $2.1B |
| Employees | 28,000 |
| Cash | $180M |
| Debt | $890M |

**Fleet Composition:**
| Type | Count | Avg Age | Notes |
|------|-------|---------|-------|
| Boeing 747-100 | 33 | 6 yrs | Flagship, but overcapacity |
| Boeing 707-320B | 42 | 14 yrs | Aging, fuel inefficient |
| Boeing 727-200 | 28 | 8 yrs | Domestic routes |
| Boeing 737-200 | 12 | 3 yrs | Shuttle services |
| L-1011-500 | 12 | 2 yrs | Recent delivery |

**Network:**
- International: 86 destinations, 55 countries
- Domestic: Limited (no domestic feed)
- Key routes: JFK-LHR, JFK-CDG, JFK-TYO, MIA-South America

**Historical Context:**
- Just lost domestic routes to competition (no CAB protection)
- Deregulation Act signed October 1978
- Fuel prices volatile (second oil crisis coming 1979)
- No hub-and-spoke system, point-to-point model outdated
- Brand prestige high but eroding

**Key Events (Historical Timeline):**
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1979 | Second oil crisis, fuel costs spike 150% |
| 1980 | Sells Pacific routes to United for $750M |
| 1981 | Air traffic controller strike |
| 1982 | Recession hits international travel |
| 1985 | Sells hotels (InterContinental) |
| 1988 | Lockerbie bombing (Flight 103) |
| 1991 | Bankruptcy, Delta acquires Atlantic routes |

**Player Challenge:**
- Build domestic feed network before deregulated competitors
- Manage 747 overcapacity (sell? Convert to cargo?)
- Navigate fuel crisis without bankruptcy
- Preserve brand while cutting costs

**Victory Conditions:**
| Tier | Condition |
|------|-----------|
| Bronze | Survive to 1990 |
| Silver | Profitable in 1985 |
| Gold | Still operating independently in 2000 |
